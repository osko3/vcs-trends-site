Key Milestones in VCS History

Version control has evolved significantly over the past few decades. Below is a brief timeline of major systems that shaped its development:

  1. RCS (1982): One of the earliest systems for managing revisions on single files.
  2. CVS (1990): Introduced collaboration features for teams, but lacked good branching support.
  3. Subversion (SVN) (2000): Improved over CVS with better support for directories and branching.
  4. Git (2005): Created by Linus Torvalds; fully distributed and highly performant.
  5. Mercurial (2005): Similar to Git, with a focus on simplicity and usability.

The move from centralized to distributed systems transformed the way teams work together on code.
